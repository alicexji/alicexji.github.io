Escape

Portfolio Investigation: How are two worlds connected? What role does string play in connecting those worlds?

Idea: This piece is inspired by a fire hydrant that I see on the side of the road when I walk home. It’s so old that it is definitely unusable. This image depicts the inside of the hydrant, with overgrown aquarium plants floating in water, versus the outside world that is on fire. The abandonment of the fire hydrant means that the water and fire will never meet. Instead, the only escape from the burning buildings are the rope ladders (reminiscent of firefighters), that lead up to the sky, and exploring the strings that connect the worlds of life and death.

Process: The balloon sleeves are designed to mimic the shape of the fire hydrant bulb. Moths in dreams symbolize loss, alluding to the ascent of death. I originally wanted to use chrysanthemums but I changed it to moths to give it the feeling of floating upwards.

Morning Rays

Portfolio Investigation: How are two worlds connected? What role does string play in connecting those worlds?

Idea: This piece offers a look into the way threads of light can alter our perception, and how its disappearance submerges us in a world of darkness. The intriguing source of light is the hope that rays can pull people towards enlightenment and awareness, rather than leave them grasping in the world of darkness and prejudice.

Threshold

Portfolio Investigation: How are two worlds connected? What role does string play in connecting those worlds?

Idea: This piece is the first to show the physical threshold between two worlds. I created figures of windows and door to show the openings, or physical thresholds, from which words come pouring out. The top right section of the window shows the process of the window being translated into image, synonymous with the words being translated into text. Finally, there is a distinction between the text behind the window (threshold) and the words spilling out. Using Roald Dahl’s book “The Witches”, I jept the fanstastical elements about witches (“A Note About Witches”) behind the window, and the disturbing but real elements in the foreground (“but this is not a fairy-tale” and “Tell me about the children who have disappeared”).

Process: I covered the canvas with torn pages that I drew on. The window lining, curtains, and words are sewn on.

Lotus Weavers

Portfolio Investigation: How are two worlds connected? What role does string play in connecting those worlds?

Idea: There is always a disconnect between the world of the past and of the present. The lotus thread weavers of Myanmar are the only women left in the world who continue to hand produce lotus thread. This tradition, which has been passed down from generation to generation, is one of the few cultural remnants that tie our current world to the past. Fittingly, women of the past have used threads to communicate with those in the future through woven tales, such as the woven tales of Indonesia. From the Ballad of Mulan (which begins with Mulan at the loom) to the Native American Spider Grandmother (who wove the stars into the sky), the women of the past leave permanent reminders of the past world. Take Philomela, who exposed her rapist’s crime by weaving it into a tapestry and Ariadne, whose ball of string was used to navigate the labyrinth. String has always been the medium through which history is made, derived, and preserved.

String Theory

Portfolio Investigation: How are two worlds connected? What role does string play in connecting those worlds?

Idea: What is the true nature of our reality? String theory is the idea that the physical particles that make up our world are actually one dimensional strings. The way these strings propagate and interact through space dictate the reality of our universe. Strings make the difference between our perceived world and it’s true nature.

Sentences

Portfolio Investigation: How are two worlds connected? What role does string play in connecting those worlds?

Idea: Every day we log onto an alternate world for information, work, and entertainment. This non-tangible world known as tech is made up of basic programming language data types such as integers, booleans, and strings. When we lift up lines of code we might find the wires and threads embedded in every language.

Process: I used a label embosser to print out lines of code that I wrote for a palindrome CS lab. I glued multicolored strings underneath the lines of label tape.
